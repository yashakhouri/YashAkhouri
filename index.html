About
WHO
I AM.
"I grew up in Johannesburg, Nairobi, and New Delhi — cities where the gap between good design and who it actually serves is impossible to ignore."

I went to architecture school in Oregon — grew mycelium insulation in a lab, built exhibition walls for Venice, mapped wetland ecosystems in Connecticut. Now I'm in New York, at Perkins & Will, working on healthcare buildings that should do better by the communities around them.

I simulate daylighting and fabricate furniture in the same week. I write white papers and work with my hands. The through-line everywhere: what does this place owe the people in it?

That question came from growing up between worlds. It's still the one that drives the work.

HOW I
WORK.

I move between scales — from a mycelium sample to a 550,000 SF hospital. The method stays the same: understand the place and the people first, then let simulation and making test the idea.

01
Research + Define

Community health mapping, site ecology, occupant demographics, material flows. PRECEDE model, wetland surveys, ASTM protocols — each project starts with its own epistemology.

02
Simulate + Make

Daylighting, embodied carbon, structural optimisation, hands-on material testing. Making and simulating are the same act.

03
Communicate + Deliver

Dashboards, white papers, fabricated objects, interactive tools. The output matches the audience — from a net-zero narrative for a hospital board to a bench shipped flat-pack to Venice.
